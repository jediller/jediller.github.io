Today we are looking at the state of Montana, located in the northwestern United States and sharing a long northern border with Canada. Montana's geography is famously split into two very distinct halves. The western third is dominated by the towering, rugged peaks of the Rocky Mountains, featuring dozens of named mountain ranges and deep, glacier-carved valleys. This area includes the famous Glacier National Park and the Continental Divide. 

The eastern two-thirds of the state consist of the wide, sweeping Great Plains, characterized by open prairies, rolling hills, and dramatic badlands. Major waterways include the mighty Missouri River, which begins its long journey in the Rocky Mountains of Montana, as well as the Yellowstone River. The state's geography deeply influences its economy and way of life. The eastern plains provide vast, open lands perfect for agriculture and cattle ranching, while the western mountains support timber, historical mining, and a massive outdoor recreation and tourism industry. 

Now, moving on to our international destination, today we explore the country of Chile. 

Chile is a beautifully extreme country located along the western edge of South America. It is uniquely long and narrow, stretching over two thousand six hundred miles from north to south, but averaging only about one hundred ten miles wide from east to west. Chile is completely bordered by the Pacific Ocean to the west, and the massive, towering peaks of the Andes Mountains to the east, which separate it from Argentina. 

This narrow shape gives Chile a massive variety of climates and landscapes. In the far north, you will find the Atacama Desert, one of the driest places on Earth. The central region features a fertile, mild climate that is excellent for agriculture and famous for its vineyards. As you travel south, the landscape dramatically shifts into ancient forests, deep fjords, beautiful blue lakes, and glaciers, eventually reaching the freezing waters near Antarctica. The towering Andes mountains provide rich mineral deposits, making copper mining vital to the nation's economy, while the incredibly long Pacific coastline supports a massive and thriving fishing industry.
