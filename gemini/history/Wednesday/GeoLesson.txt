Let us explore the remarkable geography of Washington State, located in the Pacific Northwest corner of the United States. Washington is uniquely divided by the towering Cascade Mountain Range, which runs from north to south. This mountain range acts as a giant natural wall for weather. On the western side of the Cascades, you will find a lush, wet environment bordering the Pacific Ocean. This coastal area is even home to the dense Hoh Rainforest, located on the rugged Olympic Peninsula. 

However, if you cross to the eastern side of the mountains, you enter a dry, arid rain shadow. This sunny landscape is highly valued for agriculture, making the state famous for its apples and wheat. The most vital waterway tying the region together is the mighty Columbia River. It snakes through the eastern landscape, carves a spectacular gorge, and forms the state's southern border with Oregon. This river is an absolute lifeline for the state, providing abundant hydroelectric power and widespread irrigation that heavily supports the local economy.

For our global journey, we travel to South America to explore the incredibly unique geography of Chile. Chile is famously known as a ribbon country. It is a long, impossibly narrow strip of land pinned between two massive natural barriers. To its east run the soaring, snow-capped peaks of the Andes Mountains, and to its west lies the vast Pacific Ocean. Because the nation stretches so far from north to south, Chile contains a startling variety of climates and landscapes. 

In the far north, you will find the Atacama Desert. This region is widely considered the driest non-polar desert in the entire world, and its dry conditions make it an astronomical hub for stargazing. The middle of the country features a mild, pleasant climate that produces world famous fruits and wines. Finally, the deep southern region drops into wild, untamed Patagonia. Here, the land shatters into thousands of islands, deep fjords, and massive blue glaciers. This dramatic and isolated geography naturally protects the country, while driving its modern economy through vast coastal fishing and rich copper mining in the northern deserts.
