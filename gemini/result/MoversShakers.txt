Today is Thursday, June eighteenth, two thousand twenty-six. Here is what the movers and shakers are noted as saying in the last twenty-four hours.

The geopolitical landscape has been highly active, centered largely around a newly announced United States agreement with Iran. Former President Donald Trump claimed credit for signing a fourteen-point memorandum of understanding, stating that it prevented a worldwide depression and would reopen the Strait of Hormuz. However, he warned he would resume military action if the foreign nation does not behave. Secretary of State Marco Rubio echoed this tough stance during testimony on Capitol Hill, explicitly warning adversaries not to test the will of the United States or they would lose the hard way. Vice President J.D. Vance defended the administration on morning television, promising the full text of the agreement would be released by Friday and calling it a fundamentally good deal for global security.

In response, Senate Democratic Leader Charles Schumer demanded full transparency regarding the deal, calling for the disclosure of any secret side negotiations. Schumer also took to the Senate floor to slam the administration for attempting to tie the Save America Act to the reauthorization of the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act, accusing them of holding national security hostage. Late-night host Jon Stewart criticized the administration's cycle of demands and threats, sarcastically referring to the new nuclear arrangement as a masterful gambit. Podcaster Joe Rogan weighed in on the political spectacle, questioning the convenient timing of the international deal while simultaneously praising a recent and controversial Ultimate Fighting Championship event held on the White House lawn as a once-in-a-lifetime experience.

Conservative commentator Tucker Carlson took a much sharper swing at the administration, escalating a public feud by mocking the approval ratings of the former president and accusing him of globalist betrayals. Carlson even claimed that intelligence agencies are attempting to build a foreign agent case against him.

On the domestic front, technology and artificial intelligence continue to dominate public discourse. Senator Bernie Sanders unveiled a major proposal calling for public ownership stakes in the largest artificial intelligence companies, arguing that the American people deserve the fruits of this technology rather than just billionaires. United Auto Workers President Shawn Fain shared a similarly urgent sentiment at a union convention, calling artificial intelligence and humanoid robots a profound threat to the working class and declaring that labor is now in a fight for humanity.

In the business world, Elon Musk made headlines after exercising his twenty eighteen stock options, boosting his direct ownership in Tesla to nearly twenty percent. Following the recent public offering of Space Exploration Technologies, financial commentators are now widely discussing Musk as the world's first trillionaire. Hedge fund manager Bill Ackman also commented on the aerospace company, noting that their sixty billion dollar acquisition of the artificial intelligence coding platform Cursor is a key component of their massive valuation.

In the halls of Congress, Representative Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez officially launched the End Corruption Caucus to root out government malfeasance. She also confronted environmental officials over water contamination linked to massive data centers and voiced strong opposition to a new status bill for Puerto Rico, arguing it maintains a colonial condition. Senator Josh Hawley celebrated the passage of his Faster Labor Contracts Act and separately accused Major League Baseball of religious discrimination after players were warned for writing Bible verses on their caps.

At the state level, Florida Governor Ron DeSantis signed fourteen new bills into law, heavily promoting his anti-crime legislation while dodging questions about a potential twenty twenty-eight presidential run by stating he is looking forward to civilian life. Up north, New York City Mayor Zohran Mamdani celebrated the local basketball team winning their first championship in over fifty years, calling it a galvanizing moment for the city, even as he prepares to push a new tax on the wealthy to close looming budget gaps. 

Finally, in the entertainment sphere, Taylor Swift remains a massive focal point. The singer released a new song for the Toy Story Five soundtrack, returning to her country roots, while continuing to draw heavy media attention at the National Basketball Association finals amid intensifying rumors of an upcoming wedding.
